Travail d'Initiative Personnelle Encadré (TIPE)

TIPE is one of the many exam you have to pass when you are in what we call in France: "Classe Préparatoire aux Grandes Ecoles" (CPGE). It is a little research project that consists of studying a scientific topic and present the results during a short presentation.

Originaly I wanted to work on Heat Assisted Magnetic Recoarding (HAMR), a technology introduced by Seagate in order to improve HDD's data density. But, being a proprietary technology, only few informations were public and I ultimately had to abandon this topic...

At that time, nuclear fusion was a very intersting subject because it looked like the perfect solution regarding energy supply but it was also very challenging. The concept is simple: instead of breaking atoms like we do in nuclear fission we force particles to merge creating a huge amount of energy.

But there is a catch: the reaction can only be made at very high temperature. So hot that nothing can touch it! In the vast majority of articles there is a sentence like: "Scientists are recreating the sun on Earth" or "Scientists are controling the power of the sun".

There are many videos covering all different aspects of nuclear fusion.
